Life In WUXI

Twitter FacebookEmail

Introduction to Wuxi from the perspectives of clothing, food, housing, and transportation.

Clothing

Wuxi experiences four distinct seasons, with the temperature ranging from below freezing in winter to above 90°F (32°C) in summer. Visitors should bring appropriate clothing for each season. During spring and autumn, it is advisable to wear layers and bring a light jacket or sweater. In summer, bring lightweight clothing and rain gear. In winter, warm coats, sweaters, and scarves are necessary to keep warm in the damp and chilly weather.

Food

Wuxi is known for its delicious cuisine, including Wuxi-style spareribs, Wuxi-style fried rice cakes, and Wuxi-style braised pork. Visitors should also try the local specialty "Taihu hairy crab," which is in season from September to November. Other popular dishes include dumplings, steamed buns, and noodle soup. Wuxi is also famous for its freshwater pearls and yellow wine.

Housing

Wuxi offers a variety of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Visitors can choose to stay in luxury hotels, boutique hotels, or budget guesthouses. Most hotels offer modern amenities, such as air conditioning, Wi-Fi, and 24-hour room service. For longer stays, visitors can rent apartments or houses through online platforms.

Transportation

Wuxi has a well-developed transportation system, including buses, taxis, and the metro. The Wuxi Metro has two lines, covering most of the city's main attractions and business districts. Visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the city's scenic areas. For longer journeys, there are several intercity bus stations and a high-speed train station in Wuxi, providing convenient access to nearby cities.
