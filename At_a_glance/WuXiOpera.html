Discover the Beauty of Wuxi Opera

Wuxi Opera, also known as WuWuxi Opera, is one of the most famous forms of traditional Chinese opera. It has a history of over 200 years and is known for its distinct performance style, colorful costumes, and unique stage makeup.

The stories performed in Wuxi Opera are usually based on ancient Chinese history or famous legends, and the singing is accompanied by traditional Chinese instruments such as the erhu, pipa, and gong.

The History of Wuxi Opera

The origins of Wuxi Opera can be traced back to the late Ming Dynasty (1368-1644), when the first Xiju Opera troupe was formed in Wuxi. Over time, the art form became more popular and underwent several changes, resulting in the distinct style that is now known as Wuxi Opera.

Wuxi Opera reached its peak during the Qing Dynasty (1644-1911), when it became the most popular form of entertainment in Wuxi and the surrounding areas. However, the art form declined in popularity during the 20th century due to the rise of modern entertainment and the influence of Western culture.

The Characteristics of Wuxi Opera

One of the unique characteristics of Wuxi Opera is its performance style, which combines singing, dancing, and acting. The performers use a combination of spoken dialogue, singing, and recitation to tell the story, and the movements are graceful and fluid.

The costumes and makeup used in Wuxi Opera are also unique. The costumes are brightly colored and intricately embroidered, and the makeup is bold and dramatic, with exaggerated eyebrows and brightly painted lips.

Where to Watch Wuxi Opera

If you're interested in watching Wuxi Opera, there are several theaters in Wuxi that offer performances. The most famous theater is the Wuxi Opera Theater, which was built in 2002 and has a seating capacity of 1,200 people.

The theater hosts daily performances of Wuxi Opera, as well as other traditional Chinese art forms such as acrobatics and puppet shows. Tickets can be purchased in advance or at the theater.
