Three Kingdoms City

Twitter FacebookEmail

Experience the History of Three Kingdoms at Wuxi Three Kingdoms City.

If you are a fan of Chinese history, then a visit to Wuxi Three Kingdoms City is a must. Located in the Huishan District of Wuxi, Jiangsu Province, China, this scenic area is a replica of the famous Wuhan Three Kingdoms City in Hubei Province, which was the capital of the Wu Kingdom during the Three Kingdoms Period (220-280 AD).

Step Back in Time

Wuxi Three Kingdoms City is a living history museum that recreates the ambiance of the Three Kingdoms Period. Visitors can experience the daily life, culture, and traditions of the ancient people through live performances, demonstrations, and exhibits. The city covers an area of 35 hectares and consists of three sections: the water city, the land city, and the military camp.

Experience the Culture and Lifestyle of the Era

Visitors can experience the culture and lifestyle of the Three Kingdoms period through various activities and performances. There are daily shows featuring actors dressed in traditional costumes, performing martial arts, and reenacting famous battles. Visitors can also participate in workshops where they can learn how to make traditional handicrafts or try their hand at calligraphy.

Highlights of the City

One of the main attractions of the water city is the Boat Tour, where visitors can take a ride on a traditional wooden boat and enjoy the beautiful scenery of the city from the water. In the land city, visitors can explore the ancient streets, visit the various shops, and watch traditional handicrafts being made. The military camp showcases the weapons, armor, and tactics of the Three Kingdoms period and features live reenactments of battles.

Dining and Souvenirs

After a long day of exploring, visitors can sample the local cuisine at one of the many restaurants in the city, which serve traditional dishes from the Three Kingdoms period. For souvenirs, there are numerous shops selling replicas of ancient artifacts, calligraphy, and paintings.

Tips:

  • Visit during weekdays to avoid the crowds.
  • Bring sunscreen and comfortable shoes as there is a lot of walking involved.
  • Don't miss the daily performances and shows, which provide a great insight into the Three Kingdoms period.
  • Try the local food and snacks at the restaurants and food stalls in the park.
  • Consider purchasing a guidebook or hiring a guide to learn more about the historical background and significance of the attractions within the park.

Location: No. 168 Taihu Avenue, Binhu District, Wuxi
Hours: 8:30 a.m. – 5:00 p.m. (Mar. 1–Nov. 30) / 8:30 a.m. – 4:30 p.m. (Dec. 1–Feb. 28)
Admission: ¥200 per person (Mar. 1–Nov. 30) / ¥160 per person (Dec. 1–Feb. 28)
