WUXI CITY

Located in the southern Jiangsu province, Wuxi is a historic city that has modernized into a thriving metropolis. Known as the “Land of Fish and Rice,” Wuxi boasts an abundance of freshwater resources, arable land and a rich culinary culture.

Wuxi is situated in the heart of the Yangtze River Delta and bordered by the Taihu Lake to the south. This location allows for easy access to neighborin g cities such as Suzhou, Shanghai and Nanjing.

URBAN LANDSCAPE

Wuxi’s urban landscape is divided into five districts: Binhu, Xishan, Huishan, Beitang, and Liangxi. The Binhu district is home to the famous Lingshan Grand Buddha, a 289-foot-tall bronze statue of the Buddha, and the Taihu Lake National Wetland Park. The Xishan district is known for its stunning natural scenery, including the Xihui Park and the Plum Garden. The Huishan district is steeped in history and culture, and boasts numerous historic sites such as the Huishan Temple and the Wuxi Museum. The Beitang district is the city center and commercial hub, with a bustling downtown area and popular shopping destinations. The Liangxi district is home to the Lianghong National Wetland Park and the Wuxi International Film and Television Studio.

Wuxi’s natural landscape is characterized by its abundance of freshwater resources, such as the Taihu Lake, Huishan Lake, and Lianghong Wetland. These bodies of water provide an ideal environment for the growth of lotus, a symbol of the city. Wuxi also has several scenic areas, including the Plum Garden, the Xihui Park, and the Lingshan Grand Buddha Scenic Area.

Overall, Wuxi is a city that harmoniously blends its rich history and culture with modern development, making it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking a glimpse into the past and present of China.
